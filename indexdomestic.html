Domestic Cricket

Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) controls all domestic cricket in Pakistan and the national teams. Pakistan is an official member of the International Cricket Council (ICC) and is a regular participant in international tournaments such as the ICC World Cup and the ICC Champions Trophy. PCB has been criticised in the past for its lack of transparency, but it has since made changes to its governance structure.

Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) controls all domestic cricket in Pakistan and the national teams. Pakistan is an official member of the International Cricket Council and is a part of the Asian Cricket Council. PCB organizes the domestic cricket in five provinces of Pakistan. The five provinces are Punjab, Sindh, Balochistan, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, and Islamabad. Provincial cricket associations are responsible for organizing cricket in their respective provinces. There are 14 domestic first-class cricket teams and 207 domestic second-class cricket teams in Pakistan. The Pakistan cricket team is the national team and competes in the international cricket competitions.

Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) controls all domestic cricket in Pakistan and the national teams. Pakistan is an official member of the International Cricket Council (ICC) and has been a part of the global cricketing community since the early 1900s. The PCB sets the cricketing agenda and oversees all cricketing affairs in the country. The Board has a long history of dealing with controversial issues, most notably the control of cricket in Pakistan by the military. The PCB has attempted to modernize the game in Pakistan, but the sport continues to be controlled by the government.
